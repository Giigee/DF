Additive Manufacturing and 3D Printing

Additive manufacturing, or knows as 3D printing, is a process of producing three-dimentional object from digital design. Traditionally, people used subractive manufacturing to create three-dimentional objects by removing materials. Compared to this subractive manufacturing, additive manufacturing allows complex geometries while reducing material waste. Nowadays, 3D printing is utilized in various industries including consumer products, buildings and healthcare. There are differefent 3D printing technologies such as Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM), Stereolithography (SLA), and Selective Laser Sintering (SLS), with varying applications and precisions. FDM is widely used for prototyping due to its affordability, while SLA is used for models requiring high detail and smooth surfaces. SLS is used for making complex parts without the support structures.

FDM or FFF 3D printing

FDM is known as Fused Filament Fabrication (FFF) as well. FDM or FFM is one of the most accessible and widely used 3D printing technologies. In this technique, a model is produced by melting or softening the filament and extrusing a small stream of the filaments that hardens to form layers. .The filament can be thermoplastic, metal wire, etc. The advantages of FDM include affordibility, easy of use and material versatility. However, FDM is not ideal for parts that require small derails since FDM has relatively high layer height and the finished products has rough surface. Due to these, FDM is commonly used for fast prototyping and general use applications.
